What is your long term goal or career pathway?

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I have worked with people since I joined the work force at 14. I have worked with people with many different backgrounds and personality types and have managed teams. I can communicate effectivily and am comfortable with large groups on a surface level but I really struggle to read peoples emotions. I want to get better at reading and understanding other peoples emotions in a professonal sense. I really struggle to understand how others are feeling unless they tell me, especially via video or chat because I cant read their body language. Also I want to get better at asking for help. This something I have always struggled with.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include: An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives.

I have decided that my "work day" during my studies will be a typical 9-5 with the occassional overtime and work staurdays. Before starting foundations I worked out my daily routine and I plan to stick to it. Should the "occassional" overtime become an everyday thing I will reevalute my routine to avoid burnout but so far it has been smooth sailing.

What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you.

I have regular check-ins with my friends and family and sundays are strictly reserved for rest to avoid burnout. Should things become too much I will use my regular problem solving to reevalute my schedual, mental heath and workload. Then decide I how to fix/manage whatever issue I may be having.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way.

My regular problem solving process usually gets me through but if I cant figure it out in less then an hour then I will move onto asking for help from the Facilitation team and fellow students.
