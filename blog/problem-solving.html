How confident are you with each of these problem solving techniques?

  • Pseudocode: 4/10 - I am okay but I have a bad habit of not breaking it down, just repeating the instructions.
  • Trying something: 7/10 - This is my go too, however if I dont understand what I'm doing it can make me frustrated faster
  • Rubber duck method: 8/10 - Im all about this one. Do I look crazy talking to myself? Yes. Does it help? Also yes, and thats all that matters.
  • Reading error messages: 5/10 - This one is right down the middle. I find that constant error messages make me frustrated faster, especially if I dont understand what they are saying. On the other hand, they can be super helpful.
  • Console.log: 8/10 - I like this one alot and use it pretty often
  • Googling: 9/10 - This on is my go to after console.logging and rubber ducky dont work.
  • Asking peers for help: -10000/10 - I get anxious about asking my peers for help. I dont want to pull anyone else from their work just to help me. Plus its hard not to compare myself to other people and it feels like when I ask them for help im admitting that I'm lesser. Logically I understand that we are all learning and everyone has their own learninig style/strengths/limitations etc but logic doesn't always beat emotions.
  • Asking coaches for help: 6/10 - Im a lot more comfortable with this now then I was at the start of the week.
  • Improving your process with reflection: 4/10 - im not too great at reflection, but im working on it.

Reflect on a time you were reluctant to ask for help. Why? What will you try next time?

At the start of this week I was super reluctant to ask for help due to the reasons listed above. Obvouisly struggling on the same questions for over a day wasn't helping anyone so I eventually caved and asked for help. Next time i'll make sure it doesn't take me that long to ask.
