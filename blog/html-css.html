Margin vs Padding

Whats the difference between margin and padding in CSS?

17 January 2024

When I was first intoduced to the terms 'margin' and 'padding' I really struggled to differentiate the two. They do similar jobs but still differ signifcantly. In this post I'm going to go over their differences as a way to cement the information in my head.

There are a few key terms in here that I think are important to understand.

  • Element- an element in CSS is an object. It could be anything from an image to a paragraph tag. The elements are the things being manipulated with CSS
  • Property- a property is a charcteristic -like colour, font size or margin- that changes how something is displayed on the webpage.

I want to explore margins first. A Margin is the area around your element. I like to think of them as force fields. Margins "push away" from other elements on the webpage to leave a space between them. Margins can be useful for setting up equal spacing between elements or making sure the edges of your webpage are clear.

Above is what I mean by margins acting like force fields. The arrows kind of demonstrate the "pushing". The arrows also tell us that there will be at least 10 pixels of space between this element and any adjacent elements. This is indicated by the "10px" they've been labelled with.

On the other hand we have padding. Padding is located inside the border of the element. Essentially padding beefs up the space between the element and the elements border. Padding is typically used to change an elements size.
